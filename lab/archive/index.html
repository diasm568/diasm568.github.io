Serif Type Styles
Sans Serif Type Styles

Transitional

English printer and typographer John Baskerville established this style in the mid 18th century, they represent the transition between old style and neoclassical designs.

View

Grotesque

These are the first commercially popular sans serif typefaces. Contrast in stroke weight is most apparent in these styles.

View

Slab

Slab serif typefaces became popular in the 19th century for advertising display. These typefaces have very heavy serifs with minimal or no bracketing.

View

Geometric

Simple geometric shapes influence the construction of these typefaces. Strokes and character shapes are made up of geometric forms.

View

Old Style

This category includes the first Roman types, originally created between the late 15th and mid 18th centuries, as well as typefaces patterned after those designed in this earlier period.

View

Square

These designs are generally based on grotesque character traits and proportions, but have a definite and, in some instances, dramatic squaring of normally curved strokes.
